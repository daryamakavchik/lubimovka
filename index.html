Video block title

That leads us to two conclusions: on the one hand, art should not to be too close to reality, because otherwise it will not be art. On the other hand, it must still be somewhat like reality, otherwise we will not understand anything, we will not connect and we will not recognize the author's intentions. I like to think of it as a type of aesthetics - "understanding" something, roughly speaking. If we have nothing to understand, then we we will get neither pleasure, nor any feeling, nothing. And for in order to understand something, we must be presented with something that is not equal to itself. That is, we face something and we understand that there is something else there. Here we are looking at an antique statue and we understand that it is a statue, but it looks like a person. If it was absolutely the same as a person, like a wax figure, then we would no longer recognize it as art. Art is for people to guess, speculate, feel something.
